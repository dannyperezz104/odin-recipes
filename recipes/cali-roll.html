California Sushi Rolls

Description

California rolls are a popular type of sushi featuring a mild, refreshing taste with a creamy and crunchy texture. They’re made with seasoned rice, filled with fresh ingredients, and often coated in sesame seeds or roe, making them an easy, delicious introduction to sushi.

Indgredients

  • Sushi rice – 1 cup (prepared and seasoned with rice vinegar)
  • Nori (seaweed) sheets – 4
  • Imitation crab sticks (or real crab meat) – 8 pieces
  • Cucumber – 1, cut into thin strips
  • Avocado – 1, sliced thinly
  • Sesame seeds or tobiko (fish roe) – for coating the outside
  • Soy sauce
  • Pickled ginger
  • Wasabi

Steps

  1. Prepare the sushi rice - Cook 1 cup of sushi rice according to package instructions. Once cooked, transfer to a bowl and mix in rice vinegar, sugar, and salt while the rice is still warm. Allow it to cool to room temperature.
  2. Prepare the fillings - Slice the cucumber and avocado into thin strips. If using imitation crab, separate the sticks or shred the crab meat.
  3. Set up your rolling station - Place a bamboo sushi mat on a clean surface and lay a sheet of nori shiny side down on the mat.
  4. Spread the rice - Wet your hands to prevent sticking, then grab a handful of sushi rice and evenly spread it over the nori, leaving about 1 inch of space at the top.
  5. Add the fillings - Lay a few pieces of imitation crab, cucumber, and avocado horizontally across the center of the rice.
  6. Roll the sushi - Using the bamboo mat, carefully roll the nori away from you, tucking the filling as you go. Apply gentle pressure to create a tight roll. Continue rolling until you reach the bare edge of the nori.
  7. Seal the roll - Wet the bare edge of the nori with a little water to help seal the roll. Gently press the roll to secure it.
  8. Slice the roll - Use a sharp knife to slice the roll into 6-8 pieces, wiping the knife with a damp cloth between cuts to keep the edges clean.
  9. Arrange the California rolls on a plate, and serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi on the side, and enjoy your homeads California rolls!
